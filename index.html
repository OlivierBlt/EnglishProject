Our story

Every project has a moment of "wait — that actually makes sense."

Origin

"Every single day, thousands of hair salons across the world sweep hair off the floor and throw it in the trash. That's tons — literally tons — of a natural, durable, protein-rich fiber… just wasted. What if we told you we found a way to turn that waste into something you'd actually want in your living room?"

It started as a joke. One of us was at the hairdresser, looked at the pile of hair on the floor, and said: "That honestly looks like a rug." We laughed. Then we stopped laughing. Because it actually made sense. Hair is made of keratin — the same protein as wool, as animal fur. It's strong, it's flexible, it's naturally produced. And nobody was doing anything with it. So we asked ourselves: why not us?

Process

Collection, preparation, and transformation into a research-driven product.

01

Collect

We set up dedicated collection points in partner hair salons — think recycling bins, but for hair. Salons already separate their waste; we give them a new bin and a purpose. Each collection point serves as a local supply node in our growing network.

02

Prepare

The collected hair goes through sorting, cleaning, and treatment. We remove impurities, standardize the fiber length, and ensure consistency — the same logic as processing raw wool, just a different source. Our protocols were developed to sanitize and improve the binding capacity of the fiber.

03

Craft

The treated fibers are woven or felted into rugs and mats. Each piece has a unique texture and color profile depending on the hair blend — no two rugs are the same. The result is durable, soft, and washable with mild shampoo and lukewarm water.

The science

Hair is not just waste. It's a high-performance natural fiber.

Keratin fiber

Hair is composed of keratin, a fibrous structural protein — the same material found in wool and animal fur. It's incredibly resistant: it doesn't decompose easily, it's waterproof, and it has natural insulating properties. In our lab, we developed a treatment process that sanitizes the fibers, improves their binding capacity, and makes them suitable for textile integration.

Durability

Naturally resistant

Keratin fibers resist decomposition and mechanical stress, making them a structurally sound base for durable interior textiles.

Properties

Waterproof & insulating

The natural hydrophobic coating of human hair gives our rugs inherent water resistance and thermal insulation — no synthetic treatments needed.

Technique

Felting & fiber-bonding

We explored felting and fiber-bonding methods to create surfaces that are durable, soft, and consistent — validated through repeated laboratory testing.

Care Guide

Simple maintenance for a material with an unusual story.

Cleaning

Shampoo method

  • Use lukewarm water with a small amount of mild shampoo.
  • Apply lightly with a soft sponge.
  • Wipe with a damp cloth and air-dry flat.
Weekly care

Gentle brushing & vacuuming

  • Vacuum gently on low or medium suction.
  • Avoid aggressive rotating brushes.
  • Handle the surface with soft, regular care.

FAQ

The project is surprising, so the questions are part of the experience.

Why use human hairs as a material?

Because they are already produced in large quantities and usually discarded immediately. The project rethinks that material flow.

Is this a design project or a research project?

Both. It sits between circular material research, product experimentation, and storytelling through design.

How do you clean the rugs?

With gentle vacuuming and a mild shampoo cleaning method using lukewarm water and soft handling. Because the fiber is hair, you clean it exactly the way you'd expect — with shampoo.

Where does the hair come from?

From partner hair salons. We set up dedicated collection points — essentially a new type of recycling bin — and work with salons that already manage their waste carefully. The hair is anonymous and sorted by fiber characteristics, not by origin.

Is it hygienic?

Yes. Every batch goes through a rigorous sanitization protocol before processing. The treatment removes impurities and ensures the fiber meets the same hygiene standards as any natural textile material. Think of it like the process applied to raw wool.

What makes KeratinCycle different from other sustainable rugs?

Our positioning is unique: we sit at the intersection of circular economy, design, and provocation. There is no direct competitor doing this. We don't use recycled plastic bottles or organic cotton — we use a material that is already being produced and already being wasted, every single day, in every city in the world.

Who is this for?

Design-conscious consumers who care about sustainability but also want their space to start a conversation. Eco-luxury interior brands. Gallery and exhibition curators exploring circular materials. Anyone who believes the most interesting objects come with a story.
